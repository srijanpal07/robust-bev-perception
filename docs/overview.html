SECTION 01

The Problem — Why This Matters

The training-inference gap that causes silent AV perception failure
Core issue: Models trained on clean 32-beam LiDAR data fail silently and unpredictably when deployed sensors degrade continuously — because the degraded-sensor distribution was never seen during training.

Waymo Gen 6 (2026)

13 cameras + 4 LiDARs + 6 radars. Added mechanical sensor cleaning, hydrophobic coatings, and modular sensors specifically to handle weather-induced degradation. Software stack cannot degrade gracefully — it needs clean data.

Tesla (2026)

Cameras only — avoids LiDAR entirely. Commercially operating. But loses geometric precision that LiDAR provides. The sensor debate remains unresolved.

❶

LiDAR degrades continuously, not suddenly

Fog scatters 60–80% of LiDAR returns. Rain and dust storms degrade further. Hardware ages. Low-cost sensors have fewer beams from the start. None of this is binary — it's a continuous quality spectrum.

❷

Current approaches break at the boundary

Hard-switch (MoME-style): brittle — fails unpredictably at the modality threshold. Assume-all-healthy fusion (BEVFusion): produces confidently wrong outputs. Post-hoc recovery (RESBev): adds a wrapper but doesn't train the model to handle degradation.

❸

Foundation models + scale don't fix this

Large pre-trained models still require training data covering the degradation distribution. Without it, they hallucinate on inputs outside their training domain. Stochastic dropout training is a principled, data-efficient way to synthesize that coverage.

❹

Planners need calibrated uncertainty, not just accuracy

A motion planner receiving a degraded trajectory prediction with no confidence estimate is flying blind. A planner receiving "prediction: x±2 m at 3 s, confidence: 0.3" can brake conservatively. Calibrated graceful degradation is the goal.

SECTION 02

Research Contributions

C1–C4 are core claims · Ablation is exploratory
C1 · DEGRADATION SIMULATION

LiDAR Degradation Pipeline

Beam reduction on nuScenes HDL-32E: keep every N-th elevation ring to simulate {32, 16, 8, 4, 2} beam LiDAR. Physics-accurate degradation (fog, rain, dust, hardware failure) generated in NVIDIA Isaac Sim. Released as a community benchmark for nuScenes degradation levels.

C2 · TRAINING STRATEGY

Stochastic Beam Dropout Training

Randomly sample a beam count from {32, 16, 8, 4, 2, 0} per training batch. The BEV encoder learns to extract useful geometry from variable-density point clouds. Key claim: training diversity alone buys graceful degradation at inference — no architectural change required.

C3 · GATING + CALIBRATION

Uncertainty-Aware Modality Gating

Lightweight degradation-predictor head estimates LiDAR quality score q ∈ [0, 1] from point cloud statistics. Soft-weights LiDAR vs. camera BEV features continuously. Calibrated output: trajectory head outputs a Gaussian distribution — uncertainty widens as q → 0. Measured via ECE and NLL.

C4 · DOWNSTREAM TASK

Trajectory Forecasting under Degradation

Extends velocity head to multi-step waypoint prediction with uncertainty: (μ, log σ) per future position. Benchmarked on nuScenes motion forecasting (minADE, minFDE, MissRate). First work to connect continuous hardware degradation directly to calibrated trajectory output — the metric planners actually consume.

ABLATION · EXPLORATORY

Language-Grounded Gating (CLIP)

CLIP ViT-L/14 (LoRA fine-tuned) on Isaac Sim camera renders → semantic degradation embedding → gating signal. Cameras can see weather even when LiDAR cannot measure its own degradation. Only promote to a contribution if results clearly beat the geometric gating baseline. Reviewer question to answer first: "why not a simpler classifier?"

SECTION 03

Model Architecture

Temporal transformer on BEV + crop + box features, with soft modality gating
Baseline codebase: temporal transformer velocity predictor from temporal-lidar-camera-bev-fusion. The T_MODEL vs T_kf distinction (BEV steps vs keyframe count) caused an inference crash in the baseline — keep this in mind when modifying the temporal architecture.
IN

Inputs per timestep

  • BEV stack — (T × 22 ch, 500, 500) full-scene LiDAR BEV (possibly degraded via C1/C2)
  • Crop stack — (T × 22 ch, 64, 64) vehicle-centred 16 m × 16 m window, yaw-aligned
  • Box params — (T, 10) [x, y, z, l, w, h, yaw, dt, vx̂, vŷ] with camera-detector noise
  • Point cloud stats — (S,) density, range histogram, intensity variance → gating signal
ENC

Per-frame encoders

  • BEV encoder — ResNet18 (no pretrained weights) → 256-dim feature
  • Crop encoder — lightweight 3-stage CNN → 128-dim feature
  • Box MLP — Linear(10, 64) → 64-dim feature
  • Concatenated: (T, 448) sequence
TMP

Temporal aggregation

2-layer, 4-head Transformer encoder with learned positional encoding. Mean-pool over T steps → 256-dim context vector. GRU variant available as a faster baseline.

GAT

Modality gating (C3)

Quality estimator MLP on point cloud stats → scalar q ∈ [0, 1]. Fused feature = q × fLiDAR + (1−q) × fcamera. q also conditions the trajectory head uncertainty.

OUT

Output heads

  • VelocityHead — 2-layer MLP → [vx, vy] (baseline, Month 1)
  • TrajectoryHead — separate μ and log σ heads → (T_future, 2) each (C4, Month 2)
  • T_future = 6 steps × 0.5 s = 3 s horizon, matching nuScenes forecasting protocol
SECTION 04

Evaluation Plan

Three experiments — degradation curves · calibration sweep · Isaac Sim cross-domain

Primary Metrics

ADE @ 1s / 2s / 3s FDE @ 1s / 2s / 3s ECE (calibration) NLL (proper scoring) Interval coverage @ 90%
EXP1

Degradation Curves — Figure 1 of the paper

Plot ADE / FDE / ECE vs. LiDAR beam count {32, 16, 8, 4, 2, 0} for:

  • Baseline — clean-trained model (collapses at low beam counts)
  • + Stochastic dropout training (C2)
  • + Soft modality gating + calibrated output (C2 + C3)

The gap between curve 1 and curve 3 across all metrics is the paper's main result.

EXP2

Calibration Sweep — Reliability Diagrams

At each beam level: compute ECE, interval coverage at 50/80/90/95%, NLL, and reliability diagram. Key diagnostic: does predicted uncertainty widen proportionally as beams drop? Over-confidence under degradation (intervals too narrow) is the dangerous failure mode.

EXP3

Cross-Domain Robustness — Isaac Sim Physics-Accurate Degradation

Train on real nuScenes only. Test on Isaac Sim degraded scenes — no Isaac Sim training data. Conditions: clear {32, 16, 8, 4} beams, fog (light/moderate/heavy), rain, dust storm, LiDAR failure.

This is NOT sim-to-real. Call it "cross-domain robustness validation." Question answered: does stochastic dropout training generalize to physics-accurate degradation beyond artificially subsampled beams?
